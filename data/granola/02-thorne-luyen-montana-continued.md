- Multi-day drift boat trip on the Missouri River out of Holter Dam
- Focused on sight fishing throughout: only stopped where fish were visible or feeding

### Day One: Holter Dam to Downstream Islands

- Crossed and stopped almost immediately: rising fish on far bank
  - Caught one rainbow right away on tiny Tricos
  - Fished the bank until fish stopped rising

  - Fished near side and main stem; spotted one fish holding (not rising), couldn't get it to eat
  - Both tried the fish; no takes
- Scum suckers in the duckweed: hooked one from the boat, fish ran into the leaves
- Trico hatch: incredible clouds, but fish in small pods, very tough to target

### Day Two: Prime Water, Mixed Results
- Returned to the best-looking water from day one: no love despite ideal conditions
- Trico rig produced: one caught on a long cast, another hooked and lost on the dropper
- Got on the boat; drifted far without seeing much
- Stopped at a two-island section with a big riffle on the left
  - Others fishing bobbers and nymphs, hooking up
  - Caught one fish on the inside braid using an indicator fly
- Ended the day downstream of a beaver dam: dry fly to sipping fish, best session of the trip
  - Wind made it very tough: quartering left cast, wind throwing the leader right
  - 12ft leaders with 5x tippet; extra tippet added made it even harder
  - Learned that 3ft of 5x against the wind wedge doesn't work

### Technique Notes and Observations
- Sight fishing focus meant fewer numbers but higher quality targets
- Could have caught more by bobber fishing blind in riffles, but that wasn't the goal
- Swinging or mending from the bank in riffle sections worth trying next time
- Best dry fly position: below and to the right of fish with wind aligned behind
- Trico fishing: small pods, very selective; arriving earlier (7am vs. 8am) at key spots might help
- Island fish: worth revisiting at 7pm if they're rising

### Conditions and Reflections
- Heat wave and flash flood warnings in the area
- Water reportedly very low, added to the challenge
- Lots of bugs visible throughout
- Tailwaters and spring creeks were the right call given the heat
- Favorite fish: the big rainbow in the riffle on the far side of the island, and the morning fish
